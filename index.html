What is a D.O.?

There are 2 types of medical degrees in the U.S.: D.O. (Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine) or M.D. (Doctor of Medicine). The medical school curriculum is largely the same – most medical schools have students in the first 2 years in the classroom and followed by the second 2 years in a clinical setting. Both attend 4 years of medical school and go onto further training in a residency program. D.O.s practice in fully accredited and licensed medical facilities alongside M.D. colleagues in all fields of medicine – family medicine, internal medicine, pediatrics, surgery, neurology, cardiology, orthopedics, etc. D.O. physicians take into account the human body as an integrated whole; we are taught that every part of our body is connected to each other. We recognize the body’s innate ability to heal itself. D.O. students spend an additional 200+ hours training in osteopathic manipulative medicine (OMT), a set of hands-on techniques used to diagnose, treat, and prevent illness or injury.
